Notes about Bach Remedies

Seven emotional
essence groups
designated by
Edward Bach

  • Face your Fears: Aspen, Cherry Plum, Mimulus, Red Chestnut, Rock Rose

  • Find Joy and Hope: Crab Apple, Elm, Larch, Oak, Pine, Star of Bethlehem, Sweet Chestnut, Willow

  • Know your Own Mind: Cerato, Gentian, Gorse, Hornbeam, Scleranthus, Wild Oat

  • Live and Let Live: Beech, Chicory, Rock Water, Vervain, Vine

  • Live the Day: Chestnut Bud, Clematis, Honeysuckle, Mustard, Olive, White Chestnut, Wild Rose

  • Reach Out to Others: Heather, Impatiens Water Violet

  • Stand your Ground: Agrimony, Centaury, Holly, Walnut

Face your Fears

  • Aspen: feel secure
  • Cherry Plum: be in control
  • Mimulus: feca your fears
  • Red Chestnut: peace of mind
  • Rock Rose: have courage

Find Joy and Hope

  • Crab Apple: accept imperfections
  • Elm: be efficient
  • Larch: have confidence
  • Oak: restore endurance
  • Pine: have self-respect
  • Star of Bethlehem: be comforted
  • Sweet Chestnut: be optimistic
  • Willow: forgive and forget

Know your Own Mind

  • Cerato: trust intuition
  • Gentian: accept setback
  • Gorse: remain hopeful
  • Hornbeam: procrastinate less
  • Scleranthus: be decisive
  • Wild Oat: decide your path

Live and Let Live

  • Beech: be more tolerant
  • Chicory: love unconditionally
  • Rock Water: flexible mind
  • Vervain: relax and calm
  • Vine: motivate and inspire

Live the day

  • Chestnut Bud: learn from mistakes
  • Clematis: be focused
  • Honeysuckle: embrace the now
  • Mustard: be joyful
  • Olive: restore energy
  • White Chestnut: calm the mind
  • Wild Rose: show enthusiasm

Rich Out to Others

  • Heather: empathise, listen
  • Impatiens: remain patient
  • Water Violet: connect with others

Stand your Ground

  • Agrimony: communicate openly
  • Centaury: be assertive
  • Holly: goodwill towards others
  • Walnut: adapt to change
